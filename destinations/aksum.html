Kingdom of Aksum

The Kingdom of Aksum, also known as Axum, was an ancient kingdom in what is now northern Ethiopia and Eritrea. It was a major trading empire, thriving from the 1st century until its decline around the 10th century AD.

Kingdom of Aksum

The Kingdom of Aksum was one of the great civilizations of the ancient world, known for its monumental obelisks, unique written script (Ge'ez), and role in early Christianity.

Historical Names

መንግሥተ አክሱም (Ge'ez)

𐩱𐩫𐩪𐩣 (Sabaean)

Βασιλεία τῶν Ἀξωμιτῶν (Ancient Greek)


Aksumite coin depicting King Endubis

Extent of the Kingdom of Aksum in the 6th century

Capital: Axum (later Kubar after ~800 AD)

Languages: Geʽez, Sabaic, Koine Greek, and others

Religion: Christianity (official after mid-4th century), Aksumite polytheism before

Important Rulers

First known: Bazen of Axum

Last: Dil Na'od

Historical Timeline

  • 1st century – Kingdom established
  • 325/328 – King Ezana converts to Christianity
  • 330 – Conquest of the Kingdom of Kush
  • 520 – Aksumite invasion of Himyar
  • 960 AD – Collapse of the kingdom

Region: Africa

Area at peak: up to 2,500,000 km²

Currency: Aksumite coins
